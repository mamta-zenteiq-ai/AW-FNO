Title: AW-FNO: Breaking the frequency-localization trade-off in SciML by unifying global Spectral methods with local Multiresolution Analysis via learnable spatial gating.

1. Problem StatementTraditional Fourier Neural Operators (FNO) operate on the principle of the Global Integral Operator:$$(\mathcal{K}v)(x) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}(R_{\phi} \cdot \mathcal{F}v)(x)$$While highly efficient for periodic, smooth fields, FNOs are limited by global spectral bias. They utilize a fixed trigonometric basis which:Fails at localization: Localized disturbances (shocks) require a global summation of high-frequency modes, leading to the Gibbs Phenomenon.Assumes Periodicity: Non-periodic boundary conditions lead to spectral leakage.Phase Sensitivity: In complex-valued fields, FNOs can struggle to decouple local phase transitions from global magnitude shifts.2. Proposed Methodology: The Dual-Stream FrameworkThe AW-FNO architecture decomposes the latent function space into a Spectral Approximation and a Multiresolution Detail component.A. The Fourier Stream (Global Low-Rank Approximation)The Fourier branch acts as a low-pass filter, capturing the "macro-physics." By truncating the high-frequency modes ($k_{max}$), we maintain a low-rank representation that ensures global structural consistency and computational efficiency.B. The Wavelet Stream (Local Singularity Tracking)The Wavelet branch employs a Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) or a Stationary Wavelet Transform (SWT). Unlike Fourier modes, wavelets are compactly supported in both space and frequency. This allows the model to resolve:Vanishing Moments: Effectively handling non-periodic boundaries.Spatially Varying Frequencies: Capturing high-frequency oscillations that exist only in specific sub-domains (e.g., the wake behind an airfoil).C. The Latent Spatial Gate (Adaptive Delegation)The core novelty lies in the Adaptive Gating Network ($G$). We define a learnable spatial map $\alpha \in [0, 1]^{H \times W}$ computed from the local hidden state:$$\alpha = \sigma(W_{gate} * h + b_{gate})$$The final operator output is the gated sum:$$\mathcal{K}_{AW}(h) = \alpha \odot \text{SpectralConv}(h) + (1 - \alpha) \odot \text{WaveletConv}(h)$$This allows the network to endogenously learn the physics of the boundary layer—assigning $\alpha \approx 0$ to shock fronts and $\alpha \approx 1$ to laminar flow.3. Theoretical SoundnessThis approach is grounded in Multiresolution Analysis (MRA). Mathematically, any function $f \in L^2(\mathbb{R})$ can be decomposed as:$$f(x) = \sum_{k} c_k \phi(x-k) + \sum_{j \ge 0} \sum_{k} d_{j,k} \psi_{j,k}(x)$$AW-FNO approximates the first term (approximation) via the Fourier stream and the second term (details) via the Wavelet stream, providing a more complete representation of the underlying Sobolev space than FNO alone.4.
